Meyer Lemon Pie

Description

Meyer Lemon Pie is a bright, tangy-sweet dessert made with the juice and zest of Meyer lemons, known for their subtle floral flavor and lower acidity.

Ingredients

  • 1 ½ cups graham cracker crumbs (about 10-12 crackers)
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 6 tbsp unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 can (14 oz) sweetened condensed milk
  • 4 large egg yolks
  • 1/2 cup Meyer lemon juice (about 3-4 lemons)
  • 1 tbsp Meyer lemon zest (optional, for extra flavor)
  • Whipped cream or meringue
  • Extra lemon zest or slices for garnish

Steps

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a bowl, mix: 1½ cups graham cracker crumbs, 1/4 cup granulated sugar, 6 tbsp melted butter
  3. Stir until the texture is like wet sand.
  4. Press the mixture firmly into the bottom and sides of a 9-inch pie dish.
  5. Bake the crust for 8-10 minutes until lightly golden.
  6. Let it cool slightly while you make the filling.
  7. In a medium bowl, whisk together: 4 egg yolks, 1 can (14 oz) sweetened condensed milk, 1/2 cup Meyer lemon juice, 1 tbsp Meyer lemon zest (optional but adds great flavor)
  8. Mix until smooth and fully combined.
  9. Pour the lemon filling into the pre-baked crust.
  10. Bake at 350°F (175°C) for 15-18 minutes, or until the center is set but still slightly jiggly.
  11. Remove from the oven and cool at room temperature for about 1 hour.
  12. Refrigerate for at least 2-3 hours, or until fully chilled.
  13. Before serving, top with whipped cream or meringue, and garnish with lemon zest or thin lemon slices if desired.
